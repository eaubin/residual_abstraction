# References

## Primary

- Adam S. Shai, Sarah E. Marzen, Lucas Teixeira, Alexander Gietelink
  Oldenziel, and Paul M. Riechers, 2024, "Transformers Represent Belief
  State Geometry in Their Residual Stream." Direct calibration target for the
  residual-to-belief experiments; Mess3 parameters follow the associated
  `simplexity` codebase.
- C. R. Shalizi and J. P. Crutchfield, 2001, "Computational Mechanics:
  Pattern and Prediction, Structure and Simplicity." Source for causal
  states / epsilon-machines as minimal predictive representations.
- S. E. Marzen and J. P. Crutchfield, 2017, "Nearly Maximally Predictive
  Features and Their Dimensions." Source for the Mess3 mixed-state /
  predictive-feature geometry used by Shai et al. and this repo.

## Abstraction Machinery

- P. Cousot and R. Cousot, 1977, "Abstract Interpretation: A Unified
  Lattice Model for Static Analysis of Programs by Construction or
  Approximation of Fixpoints"; and 1979, "Systematic Design of Program
  Analysis Frameworks." Source for the abstract-interpretation framing and
  the classical guarantees this project deliberately weakens.
- R. Giacobazzi, F. Ranzato, and F. Scozzari, 2000, "Making Abstract
  Interpretations Complete." Source for complete shells, the idealized
  analogue of the empirical refinement loop.
- E. M. Clarke, O. Grumberg, S. Jha, Y. Lu, and H. Veith, 2000/2003,
  "Counterexample-Guided Abstraction Refinement." Source for the CEGAR loop
  shape adapted here to behavioral counterexamples.

## Probe And Intervention Tests

- Y. Xu, S. Zhao, J. Song, R. Stewart, and S. Ermon, 2020, "A Theory of
  Usable Information Under Computational Constraints." Source for the
  V-information / probe-class distinction.
- A. Geiger, H. Lu, T. Icard, and C. Potts, 2021, "Causal Abstractions of
  Neural Networks"; and A. Geiger et al., 2023, "Causal Abstraction: A
  Theoretical Foundation for Mechanistic Interpretability." Source for the
  causal-abstraction and interchange-intervention framing used from
  Experiment 3 onward.
